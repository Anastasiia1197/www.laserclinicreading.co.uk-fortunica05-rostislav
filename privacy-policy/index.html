Privacy Policy – Your Data, Clearly Protected

At https://lottiesdogs.co.uk/, we are committed to safeguarding and respecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains clearly and transparently how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information when you visit our website, which is based in and operated for users in the United Kingdom. Our practices are designed to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. We believe you have a right to know what happens with your data, and we are dedicated to handling it responsibly, ethically, and securely. Please read this policy carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal data.

What data we collect and how it powers your experience

To provide and improve our services for UK users, we may collect and process the following types of information:

  • Personal Identification Information: This may include your name, email address, and any other details you voluntarily provide when contacting us via forms or email.
  • Technical Data: We automatically collect certain technical information when you visit our site, such as your IP address (anonymised where possible), browser type and version, time zone setting, operating system, and platform.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you use our website, including the pages you visit, the time spent on those pages, referral sources, and clickstream data.
  • Communication Data: Any correspondence when you contact us for support, enquiries, or feedback.

We collect this data to ensure our website functions correctly, to personalise your experience for UK audiences, to maintain security, and to understand how we can improve our content and services for visitors across England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

The essential role of cookies in customizing https://lottiesdogs.co.uk/

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to distinguish you from other users. This helps us provide you with a good experience when you browse and also allows us to improve our site for everyone in the UK.

We use the following categories of cookies:

  1. Strictly Necessary Cookies: These are essential for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you, such as setting your privacy preferences.
  2. Performance/Analytics Cookies: These allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site for British users. They help us know which pages are popular and how visitors move around the site.
  3. Functionality Cookies: These enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation for UK visitors, such as remembering your preferences.

You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. However, if you disable or refuse cookies, please note that some parts of this website may become inaccessible or not function properly. Our separate Cookie Policy provides more detailed information.

Your rights to access, modify, or delete your data anytime

Your legal rights under UK data protection law

Under UK data protection laws, you have several important rights regarding your personal information. We are committed to upholding these rights for all our users in Great Britain and Northern Ireland.

Your key rights include:

  1. The right to access: You have the right to request copies of the personal data we hold about you.
  2. The right to rectification: You have the right to request correction of any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete.
  3. The right to erasure: You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions.
  4. The right to restrict processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  5. The right to object to processing: You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  6. The right to data portability: You have the right to request that we transfer the data that we have collected to another organisation, or directly to you, under certain conditions.

If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided at the end of this policy. We may need to request specific information from you to help us confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your data.
