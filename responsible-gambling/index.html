Identifying warning signs before problems arise

Self-awareness is crucial. Problem gambling often develops gradually. Regularly ask yourself honest questions about your behaviour and feelings towards gambling.

Key warning signs to be mindful of include:

  • Spending more time or money on gambling than you originally intended.
  • Chasing losses—trying to win back money you've lost by gambling more.
  • Thinking about gambling constantly or feeling restless when not playing.
  • Borrowing money, selling possessions, or lying to family and friends to fund gambling or hide losses.
  • Neglecting work, studies, family responsibilities, or personal relationships due to gambling.
  • Gambling to escape problems, or feelings of anxiety, depression, or helplessness.

Recognising one or more of these signs in yourself is a strong indicator that it's time to pause and seek further information or help.

Debunking common gambling myths and misconceptions

Separating fact from fiction

Misconceptions about gambling can lead to risky behaviour. Understanding the reality is a key part of playing responsibly.

Myth: "I'm due for a win after a long losing streak."
Fact: Each game round on a certified slot like Drop the Boss is completely random and independent. Past results do not influence future outcomes. The notion of being "due" a win is a fallacy known as the Gambler's Fallacy.

Myth: "Betting systems can beat the odds and guarantee profits."
Fact: No strategy can overcome the built-in house edge in games of chance. Systems like Martingale (doubling bets after a loss) are risky and can lead to rapid, significant losses, especially with volatile games.

Myth: "Playing for free in demo mode reduces your chances when you play for real money."
Fact: Demo play uses the same Random Number Generator (RNG) as real-money play. It's a safe way to learn game features without risk and has no bearing on future real-money results.
